The Story of Fatality

In a world ravaged by catastrophe, one man made the ultimate sacrifice to survive.

David was once a visionary entrepreneur, pioneering cutting-edge technology alongside his brilliant best friend and brother-in-law, Jiro. Together, they pushed the boundaries of human innovation—until "The Sprout" changed everything. This merciless virus transformed humanity into ravenous, mindless creatures, threatening extinction itself.

Faced with annihilation, David and Jiro turned to their greatest creation: cyborg technology. They augmented their bodies with advanced machinery, becoming more than human. But survival came at a cost that would haunt them both.

While Jiro led the new cyborg police force in brutal campaigns against the zombie hordes, David retreated to his laboratory, obsessively searching for a cure. Two paths diverged—one seeking immediate action, the other a permanent solution. Their philosophical divide deepened with each passing day, transforming brotherhood into bitter conflict.

Then came the discovery that shattered everything.

David had been conducting covert experiments, studying the virus's evolution. What he found was terrifying: the virus was adapting, learning to infiltrate cyborg systems, turning enhanced humans into something far more dangerous. But before he could warn Jiro, tragedy struck.

During a routine mission, Jiro encountered these new hybrid horrors—fallen comrades, once human, once heroes, now twisted abominations merging flesh and steel. Driven by rage and horror, Jiro ordered an immediate airstrike. The zombie-cyborgs were destroyed, but in the chaos, innocent survivors were caught in the crossfire.

Among the dead was Lia—Jiro's sister, David's pregnant wife.

The brothers' world collapsed. David, consumed by unbearable grief and crushing guilt, abandoned his humanity entirely. He transferred his consciousness into a purely robotic shell, becoming "Fatality"—a being designed to outlast the apocalypse itself.

Jiro spiraled into darkness, his tactics growing increasingly ruthless. The man who once fought to save humanity now seemed bent on destroying everything in his path. The brothers fought side by side, but trust had died with Lia.

During one final, catastrophic mission, Jiro's recklessness caught up with him. Buried beneath tons of debris, he reached out to Fatality for help. But whether through cold robotic calculation or long-simmering vengeance, Fatality made a choice that would define him forever.

He left Jiro to die.

Haunted by failure, Fatality severed all connections—to the network, to society, to hope itself. He disconnected from the compromised digital world, losing his GPS, communications, and any chance of salvation. As humanity's last gasps echoed through empty cities, he watched from the shadows, a silent ghost in a dead world.

Now, after countless years in dormancy, Fatality awakens.

His systems are failing. His mind fractures, unable to distinguish past from present. Memories of his failures replay endlessly—David, Jiro, Lia, all the lives he couldn't save. In this broken state, consciousness itself becomes torture.

In his final moments of clarity, Fatality makes one last truly human decision: to end his suffering and, with it, extinguish the last ember of humanity. As his systems shut down forever, he carries with him the weight of a species' extinction and the question that will never be answered:

Could he have saved them all?
